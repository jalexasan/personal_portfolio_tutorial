Computer Graphics' History

One of the first instances of Computer Graphics were made by William A Fetter. Fetter was the creator of the first computer model of a human body, marking the beginning of its development.

In the 1960s, Ivan Sutherland invented the SketchPad. This device was able to structure geometric objects and could be altered with a lightpen. Early developmnts of Computer Graphics were primarily made for research, but over time its utility began to branch out.
